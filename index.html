Phase 2 — analyticalWeek 5 · Guesstimates from zero
1
Learn the framework first
Every guesstimate follows: Clarify the question → Break into segments → Estimate each part → Multiply → Sanity check. Watch 2 videos before attempting any problem.
Case Interview Cracked
2
Practise India-context estimates
Examples: "How many smartphones are sold in India daily?" / "Estimate monthly revenue of a Zomato delivery partner." These are the type you'll actually get.
3
Attempt 2 guesstimates per day, out loud
Speaking matters more than writing. Record yourself on your phone and replay — you'll spot gaps in logic quickly.

The Product Folks

Study case types from IIM casebooks

Case Study Club

Phase 2 — analyticalWeek 7 · Business case studies (BA focus)
1
Learn the 4 core BA case types
Profitability Analysis, Market Entry, New Product Launch, Growth Strategy. Understand the framework for each before solving any case.
IIM-A casebookIIM-B casebook
2
Solve 1 case per day, written then verbal
Write your answer first (structure on paper), then present it out loud as if you're in an interview. Both skills are separately important.
3
Also cover DTU, FMS, SRCC casebooks
These are freely available and contain Indian business context which is closer to what NSUT placements will feature.
Phase 2 — analyticalWeek 8 · Data visualisation (DA focus)
1
Choose Power BI (recommended over Tableau)
More commonly asked in Indian placements. Follow Alex the Analyst's Power BI beginner bootcamp on YouTube — it's free and very hands-on.
Alex the Analyst — Power BI
2
Learn DAX basics + data modeling
Calculated columns, measures, relationships between tables. These 3 things cover 80% of what you'll need in an assessment.
3
Build one guided dashboard end-to-end
Follow a YouTube tutorial for a Sales or HR dashboard. By end of week you should have a publishable file for your resume.
Phase 3 — portfolioWeek 9 · Capstone project
1
Pick a messy Kaggle dataset
Something real: e-commerce orders, IPL match data, hospital data. The messier it is, the better it demonstrates your cleaning skills.
Kaggle datasets
2
Clean + analyse with Python and SQL
Use Pandas to clean, write SQL queries to answer 3 specific business questions you define upfront. Document everything in a Jupyter Notebook.
3
Build a Power BI dashboard on top of it
This single end-to-end project covers Python + SQL + visualisation — and becomes your strongest resume bullet point.
Phase 3 — portfolioWeek 10 · Product teardown deck
1
Pick one app you use heavily
Swiggy, CRED, Zepto, PhonePe — something you genuinely understand as a user. Avoid copying existing teardowns.
2
Structure: user journey → pain points → metrics → fix
A 5-7 slide deck is ideal. Cover: who the user is, what their goal is, where they drop off, what metric is affected, what your proposed change is.
3
Add to your resume and LinkedIn
Upload to Notion or Canva and share the link on LinkedIn. Interviewers genuinely check this before PA/PM rounds.
Phase 3 — portfolioWeek 11 · Mock interview blitz
1
Daily mock with a friend — 45 min sessions
One person is interviewer, one is candidate. Rotate. Cover 1 guesstimate + 1 case study per session. Use a timer — 20 min per case.
2
Watch your weakest case type 3x more
If market entry cases feel shaky, do 3 market entry cases before moving on. Don't spread time equally — fix the gaps.
3
Record at least 2 sessions
Play them back and note filler words, pacing, and whether your structure was actually clear to someone who doesn't know the topic.
Phase 3 — portfolioWeek 12 · Resume + HR prep
1
Rewrite resume with STAR bullets
Every experience bullet should follow Situation → Task → Action → Result. Quantify wherever possible. Your capstone project gets its own bullet with metrics.
2
Prepare 5 core HR answers
Tell me about yourself / Why this role? / A time you solved a problem with data / Your biggest weakness / Where do you see yourself in 3 years.
3
Re-read every case and guesstimate you got wrong
The day before any interview, re-read your "mistakes notebook" — not new material. Revising errors is more valuable than learning new content at this stage.
Every dayThe daily execution loop

Use this loop every time you sit down with a new topic. It prevents "tutorial hell" — the trap of watching endlessly without retaining anything.

20%
Consume theory
Watch the video or read the chapter. Don't take heavy notes — just grasp the concept.
30%
Replicate the example
Follow the instructor exactly. Write the same SQL query. Draw the same market-sizing tree. Your hands must do the work.
40%
Blind application
Close the tutorial. Open a fresh, unseen problem. Attempt it entirely from scratch. This is where real learning happens.
10%
Write the gap
Note down the one specific thing you got wrong or got stuck on. Review that note before your next session.
